The Key Question in Phonology

Phonetics asked:

What sounds do humans produce? How are they articulated, transmitted, perceived?

Phonology asks:

Which sound differences matter in a given language? Which differences change meaning?

Example: In English, [pʰ] and [p] are physically different sounds — but they never change the meaning of a word. In Thai, they DO change meaning. Same sounds, different phonological status in different languages.
이 슬라이드가 음성학에서 음운론으로 전환하는 핵심입니다. 영어에서는 기식음화된 [pʰ]과 비기식음화된 [p]의 차이가 의미 변별에 영향이 없지만, 태국어에서는 이 차이가 완전히 다른 단어를 만듭니다.

Phoneme

Phoneme /ˈfoʊniːm/

The minimal abstract unit of sound in a language that can distinguish meaning. Represented in slashes: /p/, /b/, /t/…

Three related concepts

Phone [brackets]

Any actual, physical speech sound. Language-independent.

Phoneme /slashes/

Abstract sound category in a specific language. Meaning-distinguishing.

Allophone [brackets]

A variant of a phoneme. Same phoneme, different surface realization.

English example

Phoneme: /p/

Allophones:

[pʰ]word-initially: pin
[p]after /s/: spin
[p̚]word-finally: map

All three are allophones of /p/ — never change meaning.

음소(phoneme)는 추상적 범주이고, 이음(allophone)은 그 범주의 구체적 실현형입니다. /p/라는 음소는 환경에 따라 [pʰ], [p], [p̚]으로 다르게 발음되지만, 영어 화자는 이 차이를 "같은 /p/"로 인식합니다.

Minimal Pairs & The Contrast Test

Minimal Pair

Two words that differ in exactly one sound and have different meanings. If a minimal pair exists, the two sounds are different phonemes.

/p/ vs. /b/

pin/pɪn/
bin/bɪn/
cap/kæp/
cab/kæb/

/s/ vs. /z/

sip/sɪp/
zip/zɪp/
loose/luːs/
lose/luːz/
The Contrast Test: If two sounds form minimal pairs → they are separate phonemes in that language.
최소 대립쌍은 음소 지위를 증명하는 가장 강력한 증거입니다. 학생들에게 /θ/와 /ð/의 최소 대립쌍을 찾아보게 하세요: thigh/thy, ether/either, mouth(N)/mouth(V).

Distribution

Distribution

The set of phonetic environments in which a sound occurs. Critical for determining phoneme vs. allophone status.

Complementary Distribution

Two sounds never appear in the same environment. → They are allophones of one phoneme.

Example: [pʰ] appears word-initially; [p] never does (after /s/). They don't overlap.

Overlapping Distribution

Two sounds can appear in the same environment. → They may be separate phonemes (if minimal pairs exist).

Example: /l/ and /ɹ/ both appear word-initially: light/right, lane/rain.

상보적 분포(complementary distribution)는 두 음이 절대 같은 환경에 나타나지 않을 때입니다. 겹치는 분포(overlapping distribution)는 같은 환경에 나타날 수 있을 때입니다. 겹치는 분포 + 최소 대립쌍 = 별개의 음소.

The Superman / Clark Kent Analogy

Phoneme = Superman

The underlying identity — abstract, theoretical. We write it in /slashes/.

Example: /p/ = the phoneme

Allophones = Clark Kent & Superman in costume

Different surface appearances of the same underlying identity. We write them in [brackets].

[pʰ] · [p] · [p̚] = allophones of /p/

Key insight: Native speakers perceive allophones as "the same sound" — just as people in Metropolis perceive Clark Kent and Superman as two different people, even though they are actually the same.

English speakers know pin and spin use "the same /p/" — even though physically they are different [pʰ] vs. [p].

이 비유는 교재(Yavaş)에서 사용된 것입니다. 학생들에게 "Superman이 Clark Kent 복장을 하고 있어도 같은 사람이듯, 영어의 [pʰ]과 [p]은 같은 음소 /p/의 다른 모습"이라고 설명하세요.

Phonemic Analysis — Step by Step

Step 1: Phonetic similarity

Are the two sounds phonetically similar? (Share most features.) If not, they are almost certainly different phonemes.

Step 2: Distribution

List the environments (positions) where each sound occurs. Are they in complementary or overlapping distribution?

Step 3: Determine status

• Complementary distribution + phonetically similar → allophones of one phoneme
• Overlapping distribution + minimal pairs → separate phonemes

Step 4: Write the rule

State which allophone appears in which environment.

이 4단계 분석 절차는 음운론 과제의 핵심입니다. 학생들이 이 절차를 따르면 음운 분석 문제를 체계적으로 풀 수 있습니다. 다음 슬라이드에서 실제 예시를 보겠습니다.

Worked Example: English /p/

Data

WordIPAPosition of /p/
pin[pʰɪn]word-initial
spin[spɪn]after /s/
map[mæp̚]word-final
upper[ˈʌpɚ]medial (unstressed)
apply[əˈpʰlaɪ]before stressed vowel

Analysis

Step 1: [pʰ], [p], [p̚] are all voiceless bilabial stops → phonetically similar ✓

Step 2: Environments don't overlap:
[pʰ] = syllable-initial before stressed V
[p] = after /s/
[p̚] = syllable-final

Step 3: Complementary distribution → allophones of /p/

/p/ → [pʰ] / # ___ V́
/p/ → [p̚] / ___ #, C
/p/ → [p] / elsewhere
이 예시를 단계별로 천천히 설명하세요. 특히 "상보적 분포" 확인 방법인 환경 목록 작성 → 겹치는 환경 없음 확인 → 이음 결론 도출의 과정을 명확히 보여주세요.

Practice

Analysis Activity

Are /l/ and /ɹ/ allophones of one phoneme, or separate phonemes in English?

Given data

light[laɪt]
right[ɹaɪt]
lane[leɪn]
rain[ɹeɪn]
lead[liːd]
read[ɹiːd]
Answer: Both /l/ and /ɹ/ appear word-initially before vowels (overlapping distribution). Minimal pairs exist (light/right, lane/rain). → They are separate phonemes in English. /l/ ≠ /ɹ/
학생들이 먼저 4단계 절차를 따라 스스로 분석해보게 하세요. Fragment를 클릭해 정답을 공개합니다. /l/과 /ɹ/은 한국어에서 동일한 음소(/ㄹ/)의 이음으로 처리되지만, 영어에서는 별개의 음소입니다. 이것이 한국어 화자가 l과 r을 구분하기 어려운 이유입니다!

Summary

Key Concepts — Week 4

  • Phoneme: abstract, meaning-distinguishing sound unit; written in /slashes/
  • Phone: any physical sound; Allophone: variant of a phoneme
  • Minimal pair → two sounds are separate phonemes
  • Complementary distribution → allophones of one phoneme
  • Overlapping distribution → possibly separate phonemes (check for minimal pairs)
  • Analysis: phonetic similarity → distribution check → conclusion → rule
